Three dimensional topological insulators are those which have insulating bulk and conducting surface states. We synthesize these materials using the technique of molecular beam epitaxy and engineer the properties to realize novel electronic and thermal states.

Superconductors

Superconductors are materials that exhibit zero electrical resistance and perfect diamagnetism below a critical transition temperature. Our work focuses on fabricating micro- and nano-scale devices from these materials, enabling control and manipulation of their quantum states using electrical gating and magnetic fields.

Interfaces of Quantum Materials

One way to engineer material properties is to use the concept of proximity coupling. We interface materials of different kinds and study the emergent interfacial properties.

For instance, hybrids of superconductors and magnetic materials: although superconductors and ferromagnets are opposite phenomena in nature, triplet superconductivity allows for their coexistence.

Likewise, surface state properties of topological insulators can be tuned using magnetization in TI/magnetic insulator hybrids. We investigate electronic properties of nano-devices fabricated from such hybrid systems.
